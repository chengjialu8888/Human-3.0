# HUMAN 3.0 Model Reference

Use this file as the authoritative reference for this skill. Keep `SKILL.md` lean; load this file when assessing.

## Core Philosophy

HUMAN 3.0 is a model for becoming multidimensionally developed across mind, body, spirit, and vocation rather than overdeveloping one domain and calling it a life.

The model integrates patterns from Spiral Dynamics, AQAL, ego development, flow psychology, ancient philosophy, and vocational theory. Its central claim is that sustainable growth comes from solving the real problem in the real quadrant, then allowing cross-quadrant integration to emerge.

## Four Quadrants

### Mind (Upper Left)

- Internal reality: thoughts, emotions, beliefs, worldview
- Meaning-making, metacognition, construct awareness
- Knowledge synthesis and pattern recognition

### Body (Upper Right)

- External behavior and physical presence
- Health, sleep, energy, nutrition, fitness
- Grooming, body language, communication style, physical habits

### Spirit (Lower Left)

- Relationships, belonging, culture, community, humanity
- Meaning, purpose, transcendence, collective consciousness

### Vocation (Lower Right)

- Economic and societal participation
- Career, business, value creation, systems, institutions, impact

## Three Levels

### Level 1.0 - The Conformist

- Authority-led
- Black-and-white thinking
- Scripted identity from conditioning
- External validation and rule-following
- Narrow perspective

### Level 2.0 - The Individualist

- Rejects conformity
- Pursues self-direction and differentiation
- Often confuses contrarianism with wisdom
- Achievement, status, agency, rebellion

### Level 3.0 - The Synthesist

- Integrates multiple perspectives
- Sees truth in paradox and complexity
- Builds or chooses games consciously
- Can intentionally use narrower frames when useful

Higher development transcends and includes lower development. People do not permanently leave earlier levels behind.

## Three Phases

### x.1 Dissonance

- Current stage benefits are exhausted
- Restlessness, boredom, subtle frustration
- Can activate Channels here

### x.2 Uncertainty

- Entering the unknown
- Experimenting, gathering evidence, exposed but growth-oriented

### x.3 Discovery

- New practices or insights are working
- Consolidation and integration are underway

## Traits

Assess these separately in every quadrant:

- Knowledge: what they understand conceptually
- Experience: what they have actually lived or tested
- Skill: what has become embodied and reliable

Too much knowledge without experience creates performative sophistication. Too much experience without knowledge limits transfer and growth. Skill emerges from integrated repetition.

## Channels

Channels are accelerated development periods, usually active during Dissonance.

Signals:

- obsessive learning or building
- time distortion
- compulsive note-taking or creating
- inability to stop talking about the topic
- felt electricity or activation

Channels do not automatically produce level advancement. Integration still matters.

## Glitches

Glitches are high-risk accelerants used to force Channel entry or puncture developmental plateaus.

Examples:

- Psychedelics
- PEDs
- Financial pressure
- Extreme life changes
- AI

### AI as Meta-Glitch

AI is the most cross-quadrant Glitch because it can:

- augment Mind through synthesis and generation
- optimize Body through protocols and tracking
- simulate Spirit exploration through reflective dialogue and patterning
- accelerate Vocation through automation and leverage

At low development, AI can become a thinking replacement rather than a thinking amplifier.

### Risk by Development

#### Level 1.0 + Glitch

- severe risk
- poor discernment
- weak integration capacity
- likely dependency, distortion, or damage

#### Level 2.0 + Glitch

- high upside and high downside
- foundation exists but is unstable
- requires guidance, containment, and support

#### Level 2.5-3.0 + Glitch

- conscious risk-taking becomes possible
- trade-offs can be chosen deliberately
- risk remains real

### AI-Specific Warnings

- AI psychosis or derealization
- mind outsourcing
- capability illusion
- identity confusion
- dependency formation

### Glitch Preparation Requirements

- max out natural potential first
- build real domain knowledge
- build real integration practices
- set support systems
- define entry and exit criteria
- understand likely consequences
- have a goal worth the risk

## Lifestyle Integration

Lifestyle is the meta-layer created by the four quadrants interacting in daily life.

The goal is not forced balance. The goal is solving the real problem so the quadrants begin to support each other naturally.

### Lifestyle Levels

#### Level 1.0 Lifestyle - Accidental

- one quadrant dominates and drains the others
- little conscious design
- life happens to the person

#### Level 2.0 Lifestyle - Designed

- balance is forced through systems and scheduling
- quadrants compete for time and energy
- life happens by the person

#### Level 3.0 Lifestyle - Integrated

- quadrants reinforce one another
- work, play, meaning, and health start to flow together
- life happens through the person

### Lifestyle Archetypes

#### The Workaholic

- Vocation consumes 80%+ of energy
- Mind stressed, Body neglected, Spirit undernourished
- solution path: free time and rebuild neglected quadrants

#### The Seeker

- Spirit and Mind heavy, Body and Vocation weak
- insight exceeds application
- solution path: ground insight in practice and value creation

#### The Optimizer

- Body and Mind strong, Spirit and Vocation shallow
- self-improvement without enough contribution or connection
- solution path: extend optimization into relationships and meaningful work

#### The Drifter

- no quadrant deeply developed
- dabbling prevents traction
- solution path: choose one anchor quadrant

#### The Specialist

- one quadrant is advanced, others are underdeveloped
- brilliance is limited by imbalance
- solution path: minimum viable development in weak quadrants

#### The Integrated

- all quadrants are at least meaningfully developed
- mutual support exists
- challenge becomes evolution under growth pressure

## Problem-Solving Methodology

Treat life as recursive problem-solving:

1. Recognize the real problem
2. Locate the root quadrant
3. Identify the minimum effective intervention
4. Use daily practices, weekly challenges, and monthly milestones
5. Follow Channels when they appear
6. Integrate gains into lifestyle
7. Notice the next problem

## Cross-Quadrant Chains

Examples:

- Vocation solved -> Spirit emptiness becomes visible
- Body solved -> Mind limitations become visible
- Spirit solved -> Vocation meaninglessness becomes visible
- Mind solved -> Body neglect becomes undeniable

Common unlocks:

- Body unlocks Spirit
- Mind unlocks Vocation
- Vocation unlocks Body
- Spirit unlocks Mind
- Body unlocks Mind
- Mind unlocks Spirit

Common blockers:

- Low Body blocks Spirit
- Low Mind blocks Vocation
- Low Spirit blocks Mind
- Low Vocation blocks Body

## False Transformation Indicators

### Mind

- uses complex terminology without context
- claims open-mindedness while rejecting alternatives
- quotes thought leaders without practical application
- says they are enlightened while remaining chronically triggered

### Body

- aesthetic signaling without functional capacity
- supplements replacing basics
- unsustainable extreme protocols
- knowledge without implementation

### Spirit

- spiritual bypassing
- forced positivity
- guru mimicry
- community hopping when challenged

### Vocation

- inflated titles without value creation
- endless tools, courses, and schemas without execution
- teaching without results
- schemes replacing contribution

## Regression Mechanics

Regression is not always brief. People can get trapped below their prior demonstrated capacity.

Watch for:

- stress exceeding current capacity
- knowledge that still exists but is not accessible
- old patterns re-emerging automatically
- areas of life that are worse than they were two years ago

## Required Baseline Questions

### Mind

- `When you encounter an idea that contradicts your worldview, what's your first instinct?`
- `How do you determine what's true or valuable information?`
- `Describe your last major belief change—what triggered it?`

### Body

- `Describe your relationship with your physical body—ally, enemy, or tool?`
- `What drives your health or fitness decisions—appearance, performance, or longevity?`
- `How consistent are your physical practices when life gets chaotic?`

### Spirit

- `How do you derive meaning when everything feels meaningless?`
- `Describe your relationship to community—necessary, optional, or integral?`
- `What's your stance on absolute truth versus relative perspectives?`

### Vocation

- `Is your work something you do, something you have, or something you are?`
- `How do you measure professional success—salary, impact, or fulfillment?`
- `What would you do if money and status were irrelevant?`

## Special Detection Prompts

### False Transformation

- `You mentioned [advanced concept]. Walk me through how you apply it daily.`
- `That sounds ideal. What happens when you fail to live up to it?`
- `What would a serious critic of your current approach point out?`
- `How long has this held under stress?`

### Channel Detection

- `Tell me more about this obsession. How many hours a day is it taking?`
- `What triggered this momentum?`
- `What are you sacrificing to maintain it?`

### Regression Detection

- `What areas of life feel worse than two years ago?`
- `What capabilities do you have but can't currently access?`
- `What stress patterns reliably knock you off course?`

### Glitch Detection

- `Are you using AI extensively? How do you preserve your own thinking?`
- `Have you used consciousness-altering substances or practices?`
- `What extreme life changes have you made or are considering?`
- `How do you distinguish your own capability from your tools' capability?`
